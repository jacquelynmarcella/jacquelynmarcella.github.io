Challenge

An in-depth accessibility audit of Smartsheet's web properties confirmed what the engineering team had hypothesized: there were several opportunities for accessibility improvements on the front end of our website, especially in regards to interactive elements.

One of the more complex findings was on the Plans and Pricing page, which is one of the highest trafficked pages on the primary Smartsheet website. The large data table was inaccessible as it read out all content at once, not providing users with an ability to navigate with a keyboard or screenreader. It also lacked a caption and used icons in lieu of text content without providing icon labels for screen readers.

Solution

Cross-site remediation process

Leading the front end effort, I helped provide recommendations for a plan of action for the team to organize our work by component and by the types of issues to resolve. There were several trends we found that were able to work with UI/UX designers to apply learnings across our component-based system.

Plans and Pricing table process

For the Plans and Pricing table, thoroughly researching the best way to incorporate accessible accordion headers into a table, I rebuilt the table’s structure as a treegrid pattern.

Because this table is in a rich text field maintained by non-engineers, the simplest way to ensure long-term accessibility and ease of maintenance was to apply the necessary attributes via JavaScript. I wrote JavaScript functions that parse the table ensuring the appropriate aria, tabindex, role, and scope values are accurate on both load and user interaction.

Throughout this project, I collaborated with other engineers, having them code review and test my work on their devices and provide further feedback for improvement. I also conducted code review on their component refactors to ensure we were all learning from the accessibility improvements so we could be on the same page about best practices moving forward.

Results

By implementing cross-site remediation effort, we surpassed our original goal of 90%+ WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. The front end team now has an improved understanding of proper standards for accessibility that should be adhered to for future projects.

Moving forward, we now leverage tools like WebAIM and Lighthouse for a quick automatic check of items and NVDA for validating screen reader experiences. We understand the importance of manually checking items within the context of their intended purpose, which an automated testing tool alone would not catch.
