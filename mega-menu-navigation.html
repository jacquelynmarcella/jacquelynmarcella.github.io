Challenge

Auditing the Smartsheet's site navigation and information architecture strategy, we identified several areas for improvement. In particular, we found that the the sitewide top navigation was:

  1. Not fully authorable in the CMS without engineering, which made it hard to quickly pivot in content strategy.
  2. Overly complex in functionality, which was difficult for engineers to maintain and caused frequent bugs.
  3. Completely inaccessible, as it was not using correct semantic markup, aria labels, and was not navigable with a keyboard or screen reader.
  4. Lacking an easy way to merchandise effectively, such as promoting the customer conference or new product offerings.
  5. Redundant in markup between desktop and mobile.

Solution

Working with UI/UX designers, product owners, back end engineers, and marketing stakeholders, we did a full overhaul of the navigation experience and built out an improved mega-menu experience that provided better structure for our site’s information architecture strategy.

The new mega-menu format has improved transitions and animations, overlays with grid layout structure, and cohesive color schemes that map to our global color schemes. It scales seamlessly from mobile, to tablet, and to the largest desktop viewports. Additionally, it is built with the correct semantic markup and aria attributes to be fully accessible by keyboard controls and screen readers.

When researching modules in Drupal, I found that existing mega-menu modules were overly complex for our needs. Instead, I relied on the Custom Menu Links core module and built custom theming for a mega-menu that served our needs. I reviewed my approach with back end engineers to ensure it would be a sustainable approach within our CMS.

Using the familiar Drupal Menu UI, authors are able to build out a simple tree of menu links for the mega-menu and add images, link styles, and merchandising sections as needed:

Results

On initial launch, we saw a 6x increase in clicks the Platform page and up to a 34% increase to other top level links.

Since the first launch, we have done a couple refreshes of the design and content structure to match our global UI changes on the site and evolving content strategy. I also led the implementation of this same navigation architecture on the Help Center site to ensure brand cohesion.

The foundation I built in the first round has made subsequent engineering updates straightforward and has given authors the ability to manage routine content changes without engineering support.
